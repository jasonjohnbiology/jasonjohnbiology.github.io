Hello Everyone,
My name is Jason John and my research focuses on the physiology of swimming and diving in marine mammals. As a grad student in Terrie Williams' lab at University of California Santa Cruz, I've had the opportunity to work with over a dozen different species in environments ranging from Florida in the summer to Antarctica in the winter.


In my research I examine the adaptations that allow marine mammals to not only survive, but also thrive in an extreme environment that most mammals are incredibly ill-adapted for. Recently, new opportunities have emerged with breakthroughs in miniaturized accelerometer instrumentation that can be deployed on free-ranging mammals. These instruments allow us to record and study how they move in an effort to understand how they maximize foraging efficiency through unique physiological adaptations. When combined with metabolic analysis through open-flow respirometry, we can also examine the energetic cost of both stroking and 3-dimensional maneuvering underwater.
