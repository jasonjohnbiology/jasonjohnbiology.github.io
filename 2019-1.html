Week 1: The season begins!

Our team begins the trek down to the ice! First stop is Christchurch, New Zealand to gear up.

After 24 hours of travel from California to Christchurch, our first stop is the Clothing Distribution Center (CDC) at the International Antarctic Center. Instead of carrying all of our gear to the hotel and back every day, we leave most of our cold weather clothing at the CDC and just take a small bag to the hotels. As beautiful as Christchurch is, we hope we'll only be here for 2 days before we're on our way to the ice! 

Our second day in Christchurch is spent training and checking out our Extreme Cold Weather (ECW) gear. Fit and function for all of our gear is very important so we test out each piece of clothing to makes sure it fits well and all the zippers, straps, and buckles work. We also need to start packing our bags for the flight. Most of our gear will be loaded and secured to a pallet for transport, so if we have to turn around due to weather we won't get it back. Instead, we have one carry-on bag containing our ECW in case of emergency and check a second "boomerang" bag that we can get back if the plane has to turn around and head back to Christchurch.

    Despite all our preparation we are quickly reminded that when it comes to Antarctica, everything revolves around the weather. Instead of 2 days in Christchurch, we ended up waiting 5 days for a weather window good enough to fly in. Our time wasn't wasted however! We used the extra days to catch up on work, enjoy a few more cups of good coffee, and walk around the botanical gardens. It's going to be almost 3 months before we see this much green again so we have to take advantage of it while we can!

After a week of traveling and waiting, we were finally on our way south again! We arrived at the CDC at 9am for our check in, layered on our ECW, and weighed and checked all our bags. After our flight time was pushed back for the fourth time however, we started to worry that we might not make it. It was starting to get later in the day and there was still a 5 hour flight, transport into McMurdo Station and orientation once we arrived. 

    Thankfully, the weather finally cleared enough for take off and we were on our way to Antarctica! We even lucked out and flew down on an Airbus so we could enjoy the view out the windows and watch the sea ice cover more and more of the ocean below us. It wasn't until we touched down and stepped out into the COLD dry air that we finally breathed a sigh of relief. We had made it!!
